Brightform builds embeddable forms for enterprises. I worked on the renderer
— the code that runs on customers' pages, where every kilobyte and every
focus ring is billable.

Two years of performance and accessibility work taught me that both are
architecture problems wearing UI costumes: the bundle got small when the
renderer stopped shipping a framework, and the screen-reader experience got
good when state stopped living in the DOM. The AA audit that followed
unlocked two government contracts and became the company's sales deck slide
I'm proudest of.
