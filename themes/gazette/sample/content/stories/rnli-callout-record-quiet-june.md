WESTHAVEN — The lifeboat station recorded two callouts in June, its
quietest month since 1986 and a number the crew attributes, unglamorously,
to flags.

The color-coded bathing flag system, introduced at the town beach and
Pelham sands last summer after a bad August, marks the day's currents at
three points swimmers actually pass — the car park, the café, and the
waterline — rather than one board nobody read by the slipway.

"Rescues don't usually start in the water," said volunteer coxswain Leah
Brand, a schoolteacher who has crewed the boat for eleven years. "They
start in the car park, when someone's already decided it's a swimming day.
That's where the information has to be."

Both June callouts were minor: a drifting inflatable (empty; its owner
was ashore eating chips) and a kayaker with a dislocated shoulder, brought
in within the hour.

The station's fundraising target this year is a new launch tractor. The
quiet month, Brand notes, does not lower the target: "The tractor doesn't
care how good June was."
