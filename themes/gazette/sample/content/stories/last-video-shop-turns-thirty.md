WESTHAVEN — Reel Time, the video shop on Foundry Lane, turned thirty on
Sunday, celebrating with a cake shaped like a rewind button and a queue out
the door — a sentence that has not been written about a video shop this
decade.

Owner Marcie Falk opened the shop in 1996, survived the chains, survived the
postal-DVD years, and then, unexpectedly, thrived in the streaming era.
Membership has grown every year since 2021 and stands at 1,400 — roughly one
Westhaven household in six.

"An algorithm knows what you watched," Falk said, restocking the staff-picks
wall mid-interview. "I know you watched it after your divorce and hated it.
Different information."

The shop's survival owes something to adaptation — it sells coffee now, runs
a Thursday film club in the back room, and rents projectors for garden
screenings — and something to Falk's refusal to carry anything she can't
defend. There is famously no algorithm section she dislikes more than
"Because You Watched."

The film club's thirtieth-birthday screening was, inevitably, *Jaws*. The
back room seats forty. Sixty came.
