WESTHAVEN — Four years after the council came within one vote of cutting it
as "vocationally obsolete," Harbor Academy's boat-building elective is
oversubscribed, with 62 applicants for 24 benches this September.

The two-year course, taught in a shed the school leases from the harbor
board for a pound a year, has students build a traditional clinker dinghy
from oak and larch — and sail what they built. Every graduate for the past
three years has gone into work or apprenticeship, several at Wren &
Daughters, the yard that this week won the town's ferry contract.

"The argument in 2022 was that nobody builds wooden boats anymore," said
teacher and former shipwright Callum Voss. "The argument now is a waiting
list. I prefer the new argument."

The course's revival tracks the quay's: the yard's apprenticeship intake,
the oyster co-op, the market's stallholder win. A generation is discovering
that the harbor is not a museum.

The school will add a second evening class for adults in January. There is,
already, a waiting list for that too.
