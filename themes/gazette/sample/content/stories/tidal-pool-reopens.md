WESTHAVEN — At 6:04 on Saturday morning, Margaret Ash, 74, walked down the
same steps she climbed out of when the tidal pool closed in December 2023,
and swam her customary twenty lengths.

"Colder than I remembered," she said. "Better than I remembered, too."

The 1934 pool, cracked by three successive winter storms, reopened after a
restoration run almost entirely by the Friends of the Pool, whose 300-odd
volunteers logged 11,000 hours of concrete work, fundraising, and what
chairwoman Priya Nair calls "aggressive cake sales." The £380,000 cost was
met without council money: half from a heritage grant, half raised locally.

The pool's new concrete was mixed to match the original recipe, down to the
crushed shell aggregate. The changing block keeps its penny-in-the-door
lockers, now taking twenty-pence pieces — inflation, Nair notes, of exactly
the wrong amount.

Opening weekend drew 1,900 swimmers. The water, as ever, is whatever
temperature the sea decides.
