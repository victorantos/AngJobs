WESTHAVEN — The harbor board froze quay market rents for two years on
Thursday, abandoning a proposed 22% increase after every one of the market's
forty stallholders attended the meeting, several with handcarts still loaded.

The rise, recommended by consultants in April, would have taken a standard
stall from £86 to £105 a week. Board papers argued rents had lagged
"comparable coastal retail" since 2019. The stallholders' counter-argument
was shorter: eleven of them stood up in turn and read out their margins.

"The consultants compared us to marina towns with cruise moorings," said
fishmonger Ted Halloran, who has traded on the quay for 26 years. "Nobody
steps off a cruise ship here. They step off the 7:40 from Pelham Point with
a shopping bag."

The board voted 6–1 for the freeze and commissioned an independent review of
the market's finances, to report by next June. Chair Susan Voss conceded the
consultation had been "narrower than it should have been."

The market, held since 1811, runs Tuesdays, Fridays and Saturdays.
