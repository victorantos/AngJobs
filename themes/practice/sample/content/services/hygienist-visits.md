Half of dental health is gums, and gums are the hygienist's whole job. A
visit takes thirty minutes and leaves your mouth feeling like a new room.

## What happens

The hygienist measures your gums (painless, oddly satisfying), removes the
hardened plaque a brush can't shift, and polishes. Then the useful part:
coaching on your actual technique with your actual brush, without a shred of
telling-off. Everyone's missing one spot; we just find yours.

## What it costs

A fixed price per visit, told when you book. If your gums need a course of
deeper cleaning, that's a separate plan with its own written costs — never
sprung on you mid-appointment.

## How often

Most people pair one hygienist visit with the yearly check-up. Bleeding gums,
braces, or a dry mouth from medication usually mean twice a year — we'll say
so plainly, and say why.
