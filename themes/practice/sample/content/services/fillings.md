A filling repairs a tooth where decay has made a hole. Done early it's a
small, calm procedure — which is exactly why we flag things early at
check-ups.

## What happens

We numb the area and then — this matters — we wait until it has actually
worked, and we check with you before starting. The decayed part is cleaned
out, the tooth is filled with a tooth-colored material shaped to your bite,
and you're done in about forty minutes. Raise a hand at any point and we
stop; that's a promise, not a poster.

## What it costs

The price depends on the size of the filling, and you'll hear the exact
figure before anything begins — usually at the check-up where we found it.
White fillings are our standard; we don't charge extra for a tooth looking
like a tooth.

## Afterwards

Numbness fades within a couple of hours. The tooth may be briefly sensitive
to cold — days, not weeks. If a bite feels "tall" once the numbness is gone,
call and we'll adjust it the same week, free.
