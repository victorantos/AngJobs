A crown is a made-to-measure cap for a tooth too broken for a filling; a
bridge uses crowns on the neighbors to hold a replacement where a tooth is
missing. Both are quiet, well-rehearsed dentistry.

## What happens

Two visits, about two weeks apart. At the first we shape the tooth, take a
digital scan (no trays of pink goo — those retired years ago), fit a
temporary, and choose the shade together in daylight. At the second, the
final crown is tried, adjusted until your bite says yes, and cemented.

## What it costs

A fixed price per crown, in writing at the planning visit. A bridge is priced
by the number of units, the same way. Temporaries, adjustments, and the
follow-up check are included — the number you're quoted is the number you
pay.

## How long they last

Ten to fifteen years is normal; longer with good gums and a nightguard if you
grind. When we check your crown each year, we'll tell you honestly how it's
wearing.
