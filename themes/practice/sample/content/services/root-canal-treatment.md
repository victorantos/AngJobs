A root canal saves a tooth whose nerve has died or become infected — usually
the tooth that kept you up last night. The honest truth nobody says: with
modern numbing, it feels like a long filling. The reputation is forty years
out of date.

## What happens

We numb the tooth completely and test that it *is* completely numb before
touching anything. The infected tissue inside is removed, the space cleaned
and sealed, and the tooth built back up. Sometimes one ninety-minute visit,
sometimes two shorter ones — we'll tell you which yours is before we start,
and why.

## What it costs

A fixed price by tooth (front teeth are simpler than molars), quoted in
writing before treatment begins. If the tooth would honestly be better
removed, we'll say that instead — the plan is for your mouth, not our diary.

## Afterwards

Expect the area to feel bruised for a few days — normal painkillers cover it.
The relief from the original toothache, though, starts the same evening.
