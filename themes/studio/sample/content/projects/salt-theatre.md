Salt is a ninety-seat theatre above a chandlery. Their spring season had five plays, a marketing budget that wouldn't cover one photo shoot, and a fiercely loyal audience of about four hundred people they needed to double.

## The challenge

Season campaigns usually lean on production photography, which doesn't exist before rehearsals begin. Salt needed all five posters designed and printed before a single scene was staged — and needed the season to read as one voice on a high street where every surface shouts.

## The approach

We gave each play a single geometric gesture instead of an image: a diagonal cut for the storm drama, a rising wedge of yellow for the comedy, each built from the same two inks. Titles are set small at the foot of the poster, exactly where the theatre's name has sat on its door since 1974. Seen alone, each poster is quiet; seen as a row of five on the high street, the system is unmistakable.

![Poster gesture for the season opener, ink and yellow](/media/project-salt-detail.svg)

## The outcome

The season sold 71% of capacity against 43% the previous spring, and two productions sold out their final weeks. The posters are now reprinted as a boxed set sold at the bar, which quietly funds the next season's print run.
