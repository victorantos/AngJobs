Corvid started as a weekend market stall with a rubber stamp and a roll of kraft bags. By the time they came to us they were roasting four days a week and supplying eleven cafés — with packaging that still looked like a hobby.

## The challenge

The founders wanted to look established without looking corporate, and the whole system had to be printable on a two-colour riso press in their own studio, because reprinting labels every time a single-origin lot changed was eating their margins.

## The approach

We built the identity around the coffee ring — the mark a cup actually leaves. One heavy charcoal ring became the logo, the label frame, and the shelf language all at once. Origin information is typewritten into a fixed grid so the founders can reprint a lot label in minutes, and a single red dot marks each roast's position on a light-to-dark scale — no adjectives, no tasting-note poetry.

![Packaging bands for the Corvid label system, charcoal on unbleached stock](/media/project-corvid-detail.svg)

## The outcome

The system launched with the spring lots. Wholesale accounts grew from eleven cafés to nineteen within four months, and the founders now produce every label in-house in a single afternoon per roast cycle. The rubber stamp survives — it signs the inside flap of every box.
