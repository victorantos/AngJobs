Priya teaches the classes people book after the week wins: Yin, Restorative
Hour, and the breathwork that regulars describe as "a nap that fixes
things."

Her classes feel like this: five shapes in sixty minutes, each held long
enough for your schedule to stop arguing with you. She talks less than any
teacher on the timetable and somehow says the most. Phones live in the
cubbies; nobody has ever missed one.

The personal bit: Priya found yin during hospital shifts as an ICU nurse —
she still works two a week — and teaches rest as a skill, not a reward. Her
Sunday phrase: "You don't earn stillness. You practice it."
