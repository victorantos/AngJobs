Rosa's mat Pilates is the timetable's quiet assassin: no equipment, no
jumping, and somehow the class that everyone's physio keeps recommending.

Her classes feel like this: movements measured in centimeters, cues
measured in jokes, and a core that files a formal complaint the next
morning. She keeps a running feud with the word "engage" and teaches what
it actually means instead.

The personal bit: Rosa danced professionally for a decade and rebuilt her
own back with Pilates after the career ended. She teaches the mid-morning
slots deliberately — they're the classes her older regulars can make, and
they are fiercely hers.
