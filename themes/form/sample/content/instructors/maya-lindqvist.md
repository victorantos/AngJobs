Maya opened the studio's very first sunrise class and has taught it, with
two maternity breaks, ever since. Her vinyasa is precise without being
strict: expect clear cues, long exhales, and one honest arm balance a month.

Her classes feel like this: you arrive half-asleep, you move for fifty
minutes without once wondering what to do next, and you leave having
accidentally done your hardest practice of the week.

The personal bit: Maya came to yoga as a competitive swimmer with two blown
shoulders, which is why her sequences are kind to shoulders and why she has
zero patience for "no pain, no gain" — hers is the class where someone
finally convinces you to use blocks.
