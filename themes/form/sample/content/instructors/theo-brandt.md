Theo runs the strength side of the studio: Foundations for people who've
never touched a barbell, and the kettlebell classes for people who now own
opinions about handle diameters.

His classes feel like this: small groups, every rep watched, weights chosen
for you until you've earned the right to choose badly on your own. Loud
music is negotiable; form is not. When someone gets their first pull-up,
the whole room stops to applaud, because Theo stops the room.

The personal bit: Theo was a removals man for nine years and teaches
lifting the way movers actually lift — which is why his beginners' backs
feel better after a month, not worse.
