Denny teaches the classes people come to on a physio's orders and stay in
by choice: lunchtime mobility you can do in work clothes, and the
Wednesday session for bodies coming back from something.

His classes feel like this: unglamorous, specific, and weirdly
satisfying — hips that sit in chairs all day, ankles that run on weekends,
shoulders that live at a keyboard. You leave moving better and knowing one
new thing about why you didn't.

The personal bit: Denny is a physiotherapist by training and a marathon
DNF by testimony — the injury that ended that race is the reason the
Wednesday class exists. Nobody is too broken for his room; that's the
room's whole idea.
