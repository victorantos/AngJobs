We're a neighborhood studio with one big sprung-floor room, a rack of
kettlebells, and a timetable that covers both halves of a strong body:
yoga and Pilates on one side, strength and mobility on the other. Most of
our regulars do both, usually to their own surprise.

**The one question that matters — when can you come?** The
[schedule](/schedule/) has the whole week. Your first class is free: no
form-filling website, no sales tour, just come ten minutes early and say
hi at the desk.

## How it works here

No contracts and no mirrors — you buy a class pass, and you watch your own
body instead of a reflection of it. Classes cap at sixteen so the
[instructors](/instructors/) actually see you; beginners are announced to
no one and adjusted kindly. Mats, blocks, and bells all live here — bring
yourself and a water bottle.

New to everything? Start with Foundations of Strength or Slow Flow — both
built for first-timers — then ask Denny what your desk has been doing to
your hips. He's been waiting.
