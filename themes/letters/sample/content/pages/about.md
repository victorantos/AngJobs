I'm the person behind these letters. By day I edit other people's words; the
letters are where I find out what my own think they're doing.

Each one is written under the same small rules: it must be about one thing, it
must be finished before it's sent, and it must earn its length — most run
seven hundred words because that's a cup of coffee, not because anyone's
counting. There is no schedule beyond *when it's done*, which keeps both of us
honest.

The name is literal. These are written the way letters used to be — to one
reader, with the day's actual weather in them — and then sent to everyone who
asked. If you'd like them in your inbox, the [subscribe page](/subscribe/)
takes ten seconds. If you'd rather just visit, the [archive](/blog/) keeps
everything, oldest to newest, like a shoebox that alphabetizes itself.

To reply — and you can, every letter takes replies — just answer the email.
I read all of it, and the best letters usually started as someone's answer.
