WESTHAVEN — The town's next ferry will be built within sight of its route.
Wren & Daughters, the fourth-generation boatyard on the east quay, won the
£4.2m council contract on Tuesday night by a vote of 9–2, beating shortlisted
yards in Gdansk and Busan.

The 120-seat vessel, to be named by a public ballot of schoolchildren, will
replace the 41-year-old *Maud Alder* on the ten-minute crossing to Pelham
Point. Delivery is promised for spring 2028.

"We priced it honestly and assumed we'd lose," said Effie Wren, who runs the
yard with her sister. "Then somebody on the committee asked the overseas bids
what a callout would cost when something breaks in February. Our answer was:
we walk over."

The decision reverses a decade in which the council sent major work away. A
2024 audit found the outsourced pier refurbishment cost 31% over budget once
travel and delay penalties were counted.

The two dissenting councillors cited the yard's capacity. Wren says the yard
will take on six apprentices — its first since 2019.
