WESTHAVEN — The storm drain cover on Foundry Lane, grey and ignored since
its installation, was discovered on Tuesday morning transformed into a
mosaic of a diving cormorant — the seventh work by the anonymous tiler the
town has taken to calling the Grouter.

The pattern is consistent: a piece of overlooked municipal ironwork or
concrete, tiled overnight in weatherproof mosaic, always a local sea bird,
always technically excellent. Previous works include the bollards outside
the library (herring gulls, 2024) and the bus shelter on Marsh Road
(oystercatchers, 2025), now a minor destination.

The council's stance has evolved. The first mosaic was logged for removal
as vandalism; it survived when the removal quote exceeded the cost of
leaving it. By the fourth, works were being quietly sealed by the highways
team. This week, a council spokesperson said only: "We would welcome a
conversation about grout specifications for the marine environment."

The Grouter has never claimed a work or made contact. The only signature
is a single blue tile, always bottom-left.

Bookmakers' favorite theories — the art teacher, the retired dentist, the
entire Thursday film club — remain unconfirmed. The cormorant, by Friday,
had its own postcard in the harbor gift shop.
