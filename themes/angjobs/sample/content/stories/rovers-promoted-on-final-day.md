WESTHAVEN — Promotion arrived twice on Saturday: once at 4:47pm when Jonah
Osei rose at the far post to make it 2–1, and again at 5:09pm when the radio
confirmed Carden Vale had drawn, and 3,100 people who had been pretending
not to listen all screamed at once.

Rovers finish second, promoted to the regional premier division for the
first time since 1998. The winning margin over Vale: a single point,
assembled across a season that included a nine-game unbeaten run in winter
and, less gloriously, March.

Manager Carla Reyes, who took the job in 2023 with the club eighteenth,
declined the champagne on the pitch. "We were part-time when I came. We're
part-time now. Every one of these players went to work on Monday mornings
and trained Tuesday nights. Pour it on them."

Osei, a plasterer, was back on a site in Pelham Point by 8am Monday. The
club shop sold out of scarves by Sunday lunchtime — in June.
