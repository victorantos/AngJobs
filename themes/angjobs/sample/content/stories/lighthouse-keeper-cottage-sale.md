WESTHAVEN — The sale of the lighthouse keeper's cottage at Gannet Point
collapsed on Monday, and with it, for now, the prospect of the headland's
only footpath closing.

The unnamed private buyer withdrew after the land registry confirmed the
coastal path's right of way crosses the cottage garden — a detail absent
from the sale particulars. The Westhaven Civic Trust, which had campaigned
against the sale, was granted first refusal by the estate on Wednesday: six
months to match the £310,000 price.

"We have £48,000 and a lot of nerve," said trust secretary Howard Bell. The
trust's plan would keep the cottage as a bunkhouse for coast-path walkers,
with the light room open on summer weekends.

The cottage was last inhabited by a keeper in 1987, when the light was
automated. Local legend holds that the final keeper, Ivor Prentice, left a
kettle on the stove that nobody has moved since. The estate agent's
photographs confirm the kettle, at least, is real.

Donations opened Thursday at the trust's office on Chandler Street.
