The last house on Pelham Road before the hedges take over: a stone cottage
on a third of an acre, with the apple trees the previous owner pressed
every October.

**The facts:** two double bedrooms, a bathroom downstairs (common in
cottages this age — hear the catch below), a kitchen with the original
range alcove, and a sitting room with a wood burner that heats most of the
house shoulder-season. Outside: the orchard (six apples, two plums), a
stone outbuilding with power and light, and parking for three.

**The area:** the town's edge, deliberately. The center is a nine-minute
cycle or a twenty-five minute walk; the primary school run passes the door;
the fields start where the garden ends.

**The catch, honestly:** the bathroom is downstairs, off the kitchen, and
moving it upstairs costs a bedroom or an extension. Oil-fired central
heating (tank behind the outbuilding) — fine, but budget for it. Broadband
is fibre-to-the-cabinet, not to the door: 68 Mb/s measured on a weekday.
