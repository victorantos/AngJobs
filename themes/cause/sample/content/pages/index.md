## What we do

One in six adults in this city can't read well enough for a bus timetable, a
prescription label, or a bedtime story. We pair each one who walks in with a
trained volunteer tutor — same tutor, same table, every week, for as long as
it takes. It is free, private, and it works: most learners reach everyday
reading within eighteen months.

## How it works

No classrooms and no tests at the door. A learner and a tutor meet for an
hour a week at the central library or one of four neighborhood rooms, and
work from materials built around the learner's own life — their job, their
kids' school letters, their citizenship paperwork. See [our
programs](/programs/) for every way in, including evening and
English-as-a-second-language tracks.

## Why it costs money at all

Tutors volunteer. What your money buys is everything around the table: books
learners keep, room hire in the neighborhoods, tutor training, and childcare
during sessions — the single biggest reason people stop coming. The
[donate page](/donate/) shows exactly where a pound goes.
