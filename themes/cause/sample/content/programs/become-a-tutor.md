Every program on this site is a volunteer sitting at a table. This is the
program that makes the volunteers.

Tutor training runs over four weekday evenings, four times a year. It
covers how adults learn to read (differently from children, and faster than
you'd think), how to build sessions from a learner's own life, and what to
do in the hard moments — the shame, the plateau, the missed weeks. Then
you're paired with your first learner, with an experienced tutor mentoring
your first term.

**Who it's for:** anyone who reads comfortably and can promise the same
hour every week. Our tutors include a bus driver, two retired teachers, a
nurse, and a nineteen-year-old apprentice. Patience outranks credentials —
we have never once needed a grammar expert.

**The commitment:** one hour a week with your learner plus fifteen minutes
of preparation, for at least a year. Learners are asked to be brave;
tutors are asked to be reliable. It's a fair trade.

**Next training:** dates go to the waiting list first — email
[volunteer@example.org](mailto:volunteer@example.org) and we'll hold you a
seat.
