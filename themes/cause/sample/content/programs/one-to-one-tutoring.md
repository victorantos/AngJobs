This is the heart of everything: one learner, one tutor, one hour a week.

The pair meet at the central library or a neighborhood room — whichever the
learner can actually get to — and work from materials built around the
learner's life. A chef practices on supplier order sheets. A grandmother
practices on the picture books she wants to read aloud. Progress is
measured in errands: the first solo bus journey, the first form filled
without help, the first bedtime story.

**Who it serves:** any adult, at any starting point, including complete
beginners. About a third of learners grew up here and were failed by school
the first time; the rest are learning English and reading together.

**A typical week:** the same tutor at the same table, which matters more
than any method — trust is the curriculum. Sessions run mornings, evenings,
and Saturdays. Childcare is available during the busiest sessions.

**What it costs us:** £4 per learner-hour, mostly books, rooms, and
childcare. The tutoring itself is given free by trained volunteers.
