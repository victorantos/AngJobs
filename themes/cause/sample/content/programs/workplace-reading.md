The learners who need us most are the ones who can't come: double shifts,
zero childcare, and workplaces where admitting you can't read the safety
sheet feels like a firing offense.

So we go to them. Workplace Reading places a tutor in the canteen or a
quiet room during shifts, with the employer paying for the room and the
paid hour. Materials are the job's own paperwork — rotas, safety sheets,
delivery manifests — which means the employer gets a measurable return and
the learner gets privacy dressed as training.

**Who it serves:** employees of partner workplaces — currently two food
plants, a hospital's facilities team, and a bus depot. We approach
employers; nobody's boss is told who signed up beyond a headcount.

**A typical arrangement:** one tutor, one workplace, one shift-friendly
hour a week per learner, reviewed with the employer quarterly on numbers
only — never names.

**What it costs us:** £2 per learner-hour after employer contributions —
our cheapest program per learner, and the hardest to start. Introductions
to employers are worth more than money here; if you can make one,
[write to us](mailto:hello@example.org).
