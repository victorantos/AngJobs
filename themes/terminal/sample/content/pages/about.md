I've been writing software professionally for about twelve years — agencies, then a decade at two startups, then the jump: in 2023 I quit to run [Copperline](/projects/) full time. It monitors uptime and hosts status pages for small teams. It is profitable, boring in the good way, and entirely operated by me.

This blog exists because the writing I found most useful when I started was never the launch post. It was the post from three years later: here's what the architecture actually became, here's the bill, here's the bug that took a weekend. I try to write those posts.

## What you'll find here

Two kinds of writing, deliberately mixed:

- **Technical dives** — real systems, real numbers, code you can copy. The [Postgres queue post](/blog/a-postgres-queue-two-years-in/) is the house style.
- **Founder essays** — what running a one-person software business does to your engineering habits, your calendar, and your ego.

## Elsewhere

The [projects page](/projects/) lists what I've shipped. The [uses page](/uses/) lists the tools. If you want to reach me, reply to any post via the email in the footer of the newsletter, or open an issue on whichever repo is nearest.
