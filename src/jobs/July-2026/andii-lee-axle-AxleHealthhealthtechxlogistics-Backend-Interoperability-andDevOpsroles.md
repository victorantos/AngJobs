Home-based care should be the default in American healthcare. It isn&#x27;t, mostly because the technology has never been good enough. Patients heal faster at home, it costs a fraction of facility-based care, and it&#x27;s what people actually want — yet the industry runs on phone calls, spreadsheets, and systems built in the 90s. We started Axle Health because we think that&#x27;s fixable, and because the stakes are high enough that it&#x27;s worth doing right.

We&#x27;re Axle Health, and we think in-home care deserves world-class technology. We&#x27;re building the operating system for home-based healthcare — the scheduling intelligence, the clinician tools, the logistics coordination — everything that has to work perfectly so a clinician shows up at the right door at the right time with everything they need. It&#x27;s a hard and important problem, and we&#x27;re just getting started.

The complexity is real: real-time scheduling optimization across dynamic clinician networks, last-mile logistics that make Uber Dispatch look simple, integrations with every flavor of EMR and payer system you&#x27;ve never heard of, mobile-first tools that have to work in spotty cell coverage in someone&#x27;s living room, and the compliance surface area that comes with handling PHI at scale — we need to build it all, and we want people who think this stuff is genuinely fun.
